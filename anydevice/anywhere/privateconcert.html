Meet John Doe by Daniel Catán — Resources for Composers

While Meet John Doe was Daniel Catán's last opera, it may very well be your first with the dedicated talent and resources the beloved maestro had at his own disposal. This site was made post haste as a centralized and trackable web-based repository of material which might aid you in forensically reconstructing Daniel's aesthetic intent. Hence, for most links on this page, you'll find "right-click" and "save-as" is the best way to avail yourselves of the documents to which they point...more so than attempting to open and view them directly inside your browser. Some files—if, for instance, they're encoded in plain-text format—will indeed open in your browser....and a few of the media / audio files—particularly if you have the proper codecs installed—may even launch their own playback inside your browser. However, due to the contraints of time involved with this project—if you run into trouble accessing a file: 1) first, try downloading it to your local workstation via right-clicking on it. 2) Failing that, contact your dedicated support concierge. None of the files below have been compressed, so please be patient while downloading any given file.


Now, stating the obvious for legal reasons only: if you download these files, you are not authorized to re-upload them to the web, nor attach them to an e-mail, or otherwise redistribute them in any way shape or form—not via hardcopy, electronic, nor any other means. Please keep your login info safe.
